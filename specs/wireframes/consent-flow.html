Parental Consent Flow

Screen: consent-flow | Persona: Parent/Guardian (not a primary persona, but COPPA-required actor) | Regulatory: COPPA 2025, FERPA

Information Hierarchy

1
Context Header (top) — Who invited the child, which class, which school. Parents need immediate context: "Why am I seeing this?" The teacher name and school name establish trust.
2
What We Collect / What We Don't (center) — This is the COPPA-required disclosure. Must be written in plain language (6th-grade reading level). The "We do NOT collect" section is as important as what we do collect.
3
Per-App Consent Toggles — Parents can approve or deny individual apps. This granular consent exceeds COPPA minimum requirements but builds trust. Teachers see which apps a parent approved.
4
Your Rights + Action Buttons (bottom) — Rights summary (withdraw, delete, review) followed by the primary "I Approve" button and secondary deny option.

Focal Point

FOCAL The context header ("Ms. Rodriguez has invited your child...") is the focal point. It immediately answers the parent's question: "What is this and why am I here?" The parent must read the full page before the "I Approve" button is accessible.

Above-the-fold: Context header + "What ChatBridge Does" section visible without scrolling. The approval button is intentionally below the fold to encourage reading.

Banned Dominance Patterns

  • No dark patterns: the "I Do Not Approve" option must be clearly visible, not hidden
  • No pre-checked toggles — all app toggles default to "on" (opt-out) per FTC guidance for school-mediated consent, but the toggle must be clearly visible and easy to change
  • No marketing language — this is a legal consent form, not a sales page
  • No countdown timers, urgency language, or "limited time" framing
  • The approve button must not appear before the rights section

Interactive States

CLICK "I Approve" button: Submits consent with per-app toggle state. Shows confirmation screen: "Thank you. [Child's name] can now use ChatBridge in Ms. Rodriguez's class." Includes link to modify consent later.

CLICK "I Do Not Approve" link: Shows confirmation: "Are you sure? Your child will not be able to use ChatBridge." On confirm, records denial. Teacher is notified that the student lacks consent.

TOGGLE App consent toggles: Toggle on = green (approved). Toggle off = gray (denied). Parent can approve ChatBridge overall but deny specific apps. Toggles include accessible aria-checked state.

CLICK Info icons on data items: Tooltip or expandable text with more detail about what is collected and how it's used. Example: "Chat messages are stored encrypted and only visible to your child's teacher."

LEGAL Consent record: The exact timestamp, IP address, consent choices (per-app), and the version of the consent form shown are logged as a COPPA audit trail.

API Integration Annotations

API Load consent form: GET /api/v1/consent/{consent_token} — the token from the email link. Returns: child's classroom, teacher name, school name, enabled apps for that classroom, and the consent form version.

API Submit consent: POST /api/v1/consent/{consent_token}/submit — body: { "approved": true, "app_consents": [{ "app_id": "chess", "approved": true }, ...], "form_version": "2.1" }.

API Deny consent: POST /api/v1/consent/{consent_token}/deny — records denial.

API Token validation: If the consent token is expired or already used, show: "This consent link has expired or was already used. Please contact [school name] to request a new link."

Responsive Behavior

MOBILE 375px Card goes full-width with 16px horizontal padding. This is the PRIMARY viewport — parents receive consent links on their phones. All text must be readable at mobile viewport. Toggle targets are 44px+ for touch. Approve button is full-width and 48px tall for easy thumb tap.

DESKTOP 1280px Card is 600px max-width, centered. Parents may also open the link on a laptop.

Persona Fit

PERSONA Parents are not a primary product persona but are a critical trust gate. They receive this form cold — via email, with no prior context about ChatBridge. The form must immediately establish: (1) who is asking (teacher + school), (2) what it is, (3) what data is collected, (4) what rights they have. Parents typically have 2-3 minutes of attention for a school consent form. Plain language is not optional — legal jargon causes abandonment. The "We do NOT collect" section proactively addresses the fears parents have about AI tools for children.
